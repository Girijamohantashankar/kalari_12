More than 3000 years ago, the Indian state of Kerala, gave birth to the most scientific and respected martial art called

KALARIPAYATTU

Kalari has influenced not only Asian martials arts like Karate and Kung Fu but many others, too, like Judo and Jiu Jitsu!

Ancient Indian Warriors, men and women alike, practiced bare hand and weapon combat techniques as part of Kalaripayattu.

But little do people know that the ultimate focus of this ancient practice is a perfect connection between the mind, body and soul.

Everyone seems to share the belief that Kalari is primarily a martial art...

MY LIFE TOOK AN UNEXPECTED TURN WHEN

I Was Introduced to Kalari

I arrived in India seeking a glimpse of an ancient healing method called “Kalari Chikilsa”.

Learning the martial art associated with it didn't interest me, as I had seen how challenging the training could be. I wasn't tough and wanted to avoid any injuries.

While most students focused solely on mastering Kalaripayattu - the oldest martial art in the world, my journey was different.

My intention was not to fight or defend myself.
My motivation was to experience an increase in my energy level.

Encouraged by our master, I not only learned the healing art but also practiced
the martial art.

AND IT WAS A GAME CHANGER

Unlike other sports that left me tired,
Kalari actually energized me!

After intense morning practice sessions, I experienced a significant positive shift.
Unlike other sports that left me tired, Kalari actually energized me.

Those two hours of rigorous training granted me the stamina and strength
needed to carry out 6 to 8 kalari treatments effectively.

It was an absolute game-changer, being able to derive energy from the practice
rather than feeling exhausted by it.

AFTER HAVING LEARNED IT PROPERLY,
I COULDN’T KEEP IT TO MYSELF

I knew I had to share it with more people

Kalari is not easily accessible to everyone, not even in India. And even among those who have access to it, it is mostly seen as just a martial art.

After experiencing my own transformation, I became determined to assist others in discovering the same within themselves.

My goal is to help them understand the Kalari Way of Life, embrace it, and become the best versions of themselves.

Traditionally, Kalari students used to live with their master, immersing themselves in his way of life through constant observation. But when I started teaching students in Germany, I felt limited by the few hours of training we had each week. It wasn't enough to teach them the true essence of Kalari.

This made me even more determined to find a better, more efficient way to share the incredible transformation of Kalari. That's why I began breaking it down into fundamental steps.

Step by step, I developed a system that enables anyone, regardless of their
location, to learn the Kalari Way of Life.

It's not about spending hours practicing each day.
It's about making sustainable, small changes and adjustments to your lifestyle.
These changes transform the way you think, the way you feel, and ultimately, who
you become.

The martial art is just the beginning of the transformation. The system I created
taps into ancient wisdom, guiding us like a compass instead of strict rules.

And today I am excited to announce that the "system" is ready for release.
